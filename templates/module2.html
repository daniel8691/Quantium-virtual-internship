The client outlined the second task which will look at the effect of product layouts in purchase behavior and sales. The client has selected three stores which have trialed the new layout for a chips category and they are stores 77, 86, and 88. The trial period was from the start of February 2019 to the end of April 2019. To measure the impact of the new store layouts on sales during the period, we first need to select a control store from the dataset. Control stores will be stores that have similar performance to the trial store. The performance indicators used here will be monthly overall sales revenue, monthly number of customers, and the monthly number of transactions per customer. We should consider stores that have sales EVERY MONTH over July 2018 to June 2019.

Using the analysis based on the three metrics mentioned, determine a suitable control store and rank how similar each potential control store is to the trial store. We can do this by working out either how correlated the store performances are, or a combination metric that considers how different each potential control store is to the trial store in every month. Once control stores are selected for each of the trial stores, assess the impact of the trial on sales. We want to check if the trial stores have significantly different results to the control stores.
